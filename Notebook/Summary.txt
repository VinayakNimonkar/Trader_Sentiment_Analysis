## Objective

The objective of this analysis was to understand how Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear vs Greed) affects trader performance and behavior.

---

## Methodology

Two datasets were used:

* Bitcoin Fear/Greed Index dataset
* Hyperliquid trader historical dataset

Steps performed:

* Cleaned and converted timestamps
* Merged sentiment and trader data
* Created daily performance and behavior metrics
* Segmented traders based on activity level
* Analyzed performance differences across sentiment

---

## Key Findings

1. Traders generate significantly higher profits during Fear sentiment periods.

This is likely due to increased volatility and trading opportunities.

2. Traders increase trade frequency during Fear periods.

This indicates traders actively respond to market volatility.

3. Frequent traders outperform infrequent traders significantly.

Frequent traders generated over 3x higher profits.

4. Traders adjust behavior based on sentiment.

Position size and direction change based on Fear and Greed conditions.

---

## Strategy Recommendations

Based on analysis, the following strategies are recommended:

1. Increase trading activity during Fear sentiment periods

This can improve profitability due to higher volatility.

2. Reduce aggressive positioning during Greed periods

This helps reduce risk and avoid overexposure.

3. Maintain consistent trading activity

Frequent trading is associated with higher profitability.

---

## Conclusion

Market sentiment strongly influences trader performance and behavior.

Incorporating sentiment signals into trading strategy can improve profitability and risk management.
